About

Hey, I’m Tim Wilson!

I’m a father of 3, data scientist, mathematician, and a former teacher. When I’m not jamming to Jason Lewis’ Isochronic Beats and coding, I can be found in the great outdoors climbing, hiking, and camping with my family.

I have loved being outdoors since I was a kid; my dad loved camping, so I guess it rubbed off. I have been mountaineering since I was 14 when my dad took me to try my luck at my first 14er in Colorado. I wanted to climb fourteen 14ers in my 14th year. That didn’t happen, but I did fall in love with being in the mountains of Colorado. I have climbed eighteen 14ers in Colorado at this point. One day I might finish all 58 Colorado 14ers!

Camping just came naturally as a kid who spent most of their time outdoors. I loved running and playing outside with my dog Buddy, and the two of us would camp out in my tree house when I was small. As I got older, Big Bend National Park became my yearly destination until I went to college.

Then rock climbing became my new obsession, taking up all of my free time not spent studying math and playing Halo with friends. I have climbed in 7 states, mostly sport climbing, but I do enjoy the occasional trad route. I have also had the privilege to climb in 3 countries other than the United States: Poland, Greece – Crete, and Scotland.

I have never stopped enjoying the outdoors, and one day, I might have a wildling child that is ready to take on Mt. Rainier with me! The photo here was taken while hiking with Tessa, my oldest, along the trail to Spray Park.
